I.
Standing

Second daughter of Melyra Galmir and Marcus Azane. Carrier of the Blood of Myrient. The Blood Mother. The center, at series open, of an arrangement that will reshape the kingdom.

In the language of canon, the distinction is precise and worth holding: Myrella is the Blood Mother. The Blood Child refers exclusively to her son, Myroth Locsird. The terms are not interchangeable. She is the woman who will carry the next Novan to enter the world in living memory — Lessio's child, and through Lessio, the cosmic continuation of the Rebirth's line.

She did not arrive at this position by maneuver. She arrived at it the same way she arrives at everything: by being who she has always been, in the place she has always stood, until the world organized itself around her.

II.
The Beach

The series opens with Myrella alone in the dark.

She wakes in the middle of the night to a compulsion she cannot explain or refuse — sourceless, complete, undeniable. She vaults the balcony railing of her room down to the sand below, barefoot, in her sleep clothes, with no weapon and no warning given to anyone in the house, and runs into the fog. The fog is wrong — too dense, too slow, too tightly localized to a single point on the beach. It thickens around the figure stumbling toward her through it.

She gets close enough to see his eyes. They are throwing white light into the fog.

"It's you," he says — half-conscious, hand pressed to the gaping wound in his left side, before she has spoken, before she has told him her name, before either of them has any way of knowing what either of them has just become. The bond between them is real and deep and entirely below conscious thought on both sides. Neither understands it. Neither will, for a long time.

She gets her arms under him and lifts him. His sister tracks her there at a dead run a few minutes later, furious. The fog never explains itself.

IV.
Personality

The canon-locked words are strong, independent, loyal. The character on the page is more specific than that. Myrella is a person whose composure is not maintained — it is structural. She does not perform calm. She is calm. The system runs underneath every other quality she displays, and the other qualities — the sharp competence, the dry humor in family settings, the unsentimental affection for her sisters — sit on top of it without disturbing it.

This is the trait that allows her to adapt to the coexistence arrangement with Lessio significantly better than Holly does. It is not situational ease. It is not the bond making things emotionally simpler. She handles it better because of who she is. The composure was always going to be the thing that carried her through the parts of the arrangement no one could prepare her for.

Underneath the composure: a capacity for ruthlessness when Lessio is perceived to be threatened. The household has seen flashes of it. The kingdom has not yet.

V.
The Blood of Myrient

The gift skipped Myra. It surfaced in Myrella. The household understood what that meant within the first months of her training.

She carries the Blood of Myrient with the same baseline composure she carries everything else. In combat, the gift expresses as exceptional combat perception — the ability to anticipate an enemy's attack or block before they have moved. Combined with high Apex raw power, the result is a fighter whose work looks, to anyone watching, almost lazy. She does not appear to react. She appears to already be where the fight has not yet arrived.

The gift is the structural reason Myroth — when he comes — will be Novan rather than Apex. It is the structural reason she sits at the center of the arrangement that will reshape the kingdom. And it is completely absent from Maia's entire branch, including her cousin Holly. That asymmetry is not narratively explained at series start, but it is felt — by both women — without being named.

✦ ✦ ✦
VI.
The Bond

Myrella and Lessio share an unnamed biological-arcane bond operating below conscious thought on both sides. She is the only non-family member his body recognizes as familiar at an arcane-signature level. When he is in her presence, his system steadies. When he leaves Aurora, his peace degrades and his balance deteriorates — and he does not consciously connect the cause to her absence. Neither does she. The bond does what it does without asking either of them for permission.

It will not be narratively explained for a long time. The series will hold it as the thing under the thing under the thing for many issues to come. By the time anyone in-world has the language for what it is — that language does not currently exist — both of them will have already been operating inside it for years.

For now: Holly perceives Myrella as a threat instinctively but cannot name its source. Myrella does not name it either. It is simply present in every room she and Lessio occupy together, and absent from every room she and Lessio do not.

VIII.
Relations
Lessio Locsird
The Bond · Future Co-Parent
The first person she found on the beach in Aurora — pulled by an unnamed compulsion in the middle of the night, barefoot, no weapon, sprinting into fog. The bond between them is real, deep, and entirely unknown to both of them. She is the only non-family member his body recognizes as familiar at an arcane-signature level. The arrangement at the center of the series places Myroth, their son, in her body.
Melyra Galmir
Mother · Matriarch
Her teacher in everything. The one who built the framework she now sits at the center of. Their relationship is entirely unsentimental on the surface and structural underneath — Melyra trusts her completely, and that trust is the load-bearing element of the household's most consequential arrangement.
Myroth Locsird
Son · Blood Child · Future
Has not yet been conceived at the moment the series opens. Will be Novan — white hair, white eyes, white arcane output. Last name Locsird per the Royal House superseding all others. First name Myroth — the next link in the chain Myrient → Myrel → Melyra → Myrella → Myroth. Twin-like bond with his half-sister Maitee. Raised as her brother in the Royal Household without qualification.
Diamyra Azane
Older Sister
The closest sister, in every way that matters. The one who tracked her into the dark at a dead run on the night of the beach, on no warning, ready to do whatever the night required. She was furious. She was also already moving.
Myra Galmir
Eldest Sister
The one who has already done the work in her own way, with Titano. The example of what the framework looks like when it lands somewhere livable.
Maylyra Azane
Younger Sister
Currently being prospected for. The version of the framework that will land on her will be its own version, not a copy of Myrella's. The two of them know that without speaking of it.
Mimyr Galmir
Youngest Sister
The one not yet inside the framework. Holds Myrella in the household's affectionate orbit — the older sister whose presence steadies a room without effort.
Holly Galmir
Cousin · Rival
Maia's daughter. Future wife to Lessio in the formal political arrangement that runs parallel to the Blood Mother arrangement. Perceives Myrella as a threat instinctively without being able to name the source. The hostility is real and will not stop. Holly's hard line: she may seek to hurt Myrella by every available means, but never directs that hostility toward Myroth. The line does not break.
Maitee Galmir
Niece-Through-Lessio · Future
Holly's daughter with Lessio, born in the parallel arrangement. Novan, like Myroth. Twin-like bond with him from infancy. Uncomplicated warmth with Myrella. The children, in the end, are the part of the arrangement that works.
Sonya-Lee Toraiyo
Queen · Future Mother-in-Spirit
Carried Lessio. Knows what carrying a Novan does to a body in the most extreme version possible. Will pull Myrella aside in the Conception Arc Issue and offer the truth of it openly — warmth where Myrella was expecting cold anger. The relationship that forms between them across the series is its own quiet thing.
