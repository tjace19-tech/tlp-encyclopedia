I.
Standing

The youngest of Maia Galmir's daughters. Apex. Betrothed since childhood to Lessio Locsird, the Rebirth. Future mother of Maitee Galmir — the daughter who will carry the Galmir name into the Royal Household.

Her match precedes the cosmology. The arrangement between Maia and King Jonzhento was finalized long before Lessio was returned to the kingdom, back when Holly was a child and Lessio was a name that had been quietly translated into myth. The betrothal was never undone. When he came back, the arrangement was already in place.

She is not an antagonist to the Royal House or to the kingdom. Canon is explicit on this. One of the good guys. Her one active opposition is Myrella Galmir — her cousin across the Azane/Vendrith branches, and the woman who occupies the territory Holly considers hers.

III.
Personality

Intelligent, sharp, socially dangerous. Thrives in public life — magazines, events, gatherings, the high Apex social world. This is not a performance. The public eye is her native environment, and she finds genuine pleasure in it. Other characters who experience public life as a cost experience Holly inhabiting it as a gift.

Politically aligned with Maia's machine. Her ambitions run through the Vendrith branch's standing in the kingdom. The elevation of her house, the prestige of her marriage, the political weight she will carry as Lessio's wife — these are the currencies she deals in, and she is literate in all of them.

Composed by default, but the composure is a structure she actively maintains, not a baseline she rests on. Underneath the control there is something capable of breaking the surface without her permission. The lab scene in Bound by Blood is the single documented instance of it — an involuntary power release triggered by Myrella's chain-wife comment, a reaction to profound insult, not an exposure of strategy. The rebound hit Lessio, the person her entire presence in the room had been designed to protect. The irony was complete. It has not happened since.

IV.
The Arrangement — as Trophy

Holly wants the marriage to Lessio. Her desire is unambiguous and uncomplicated. It is not emotional. It is not love-mapped. It is prestige-driven — a trophy. The arrangement represents status, political victory, the elevation of Maia's branch into direct Royal proximity. She does not need it to be more than that, and canon does not soften this.

What she does with the arrangement, though, is more nuanced than the trophy framing implies. She understands that comfort with her is a prerequisite for what both of them have been asked to do. She understands that Lessio will not reach toward her — that physical constancy is the only viable method given who he is. So she builds toward him. She establishes proximity. She lets him use her presence as a grounding anchor (the heel-tap tic; she does not remove it from him, whether she has consciously observed it or simply noticed that proximity works).

Ayravelle's read of Holly — recorded in Aurea-Lee's POV chapter — is the most accurate in-canon assessment:

"She's been told to make him comfortable. To turn duty into familiarity. Every little touch is an order from someone else. She's trying to make it easier for him… even if it doesn't look like it."

"It is wrong. But not all wrong things are cruel."

V.
Dominance Architecture

Holly's dominance instinct is not directed at Lessio. This distinction is canon and absolute. Where she channels it is threefold:

The Marriage
Territory. This is hers, and she intends to inhabit it fully — publicly, privately, politically.
Her Position
The architecture of her presence in his life. She is building toward being the person he reaches for when the world is loud. Not through dominance of him, but through reliable proximity.
Myrella
Rival within the claimed space. Myrella exists inside Holly's territory, and Holly can feel that Myrella is closer to Lessio than she can reach through deliberate effort — without being able to explain why. She does not know about the arcane bond. She perceives the threat and cannot name it.

The canonical hard line: Holly may hurt Myrella by every available means. She never directs that hostility toward Myrella's son. This line does not break. When Myroth and Maitee grow up side by side in the Royal Household, they will consider themselves brother and sister without qualification — and Holly's treatment of Myroth will never be what breaks that bond.

VII.
Maitee — The Granddaughter Who Will Carry the Name

Holly's daughter with Lessio. Her name — pronounced My-tee — is a phonetic echo of Maia, and an analogue of the word "mighty." The name honors Holly's mother, Maitee's grandmother, through sound alone.

Maitee will carry the Galmir name. The Royal House's naming rule — the Crown supersedes all other houses — runs through Holly's surname, and Holly is a Galmir. This is the second slot of the alternating convention as it operates across Lessio's children: Myroth carries Locsird (first, as the Blood Child), and Maitee carries Galmir (second, by the same logic).

Maitee is a Novan. White hair, white eyes, white arcane output. Arcane essence expressing all frequencies simultaneously — analogous to white light. No natural end to her lifespan; she can only be killed in battle. She and her half-brother Myroth share a twin-like bond, raised as sister and brother without qualification, with unified hostility toward anyone citing the half-sibling technicality.

Maia is quietly, privately, permanently pleased by the outcome. The cosmically-significant Blood Child is a Locsird. The Galmir name survives into the Royal Household through her own granddaughter.

The arrangement is prestige-driven on her end, but the building-toward-him work is producing something with its own small warmth. The woman who occupies the territory Holly claimed. Cousin across the Azane/Vendrith branches. Maitee Galmir
