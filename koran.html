I.
Personality
The stillness unusual for fifteen.

Koran is composed in a way most fifteen-year-olds are not. Not stiff — composed. He listens before he speaks. He thinks before he answers. When he does answer he tends toward the truthful answer rather than the quick one, even when the truthful answer is harder. The siblings closest to him in age are nearly sixty years older than him, and somewhere in his formation he learned that performing youth around them was not going to land — so he did not bother.

He has his father's steadiness and his mother's exactness. He has not yet had to find out what is his own. That arrives over the next decade. The shape it takes is not yet known.

He does not chafe against the betrothal. He does not chafe against the formal events, the council appearances, the photographs. He does not perform enthusiasm for any of it either. He is simply where he is asked to be, and he is paying attention while he is there.

II.
The Late Gift
Born with the Rebirth.

Koran was born approximately fifteen years ago — around the same season Lessio Locsird was born, when the world's quiet instinct toward new life arrived alongside the cosmic event. Many families across Gemlyria brought new children into being in those years, not out of fashion but as a kind of honoring. Khlora and Roven were among them. He is the eighth child, and the last.

The Gap
Nika, the nearest sibling above him, is fifty-nine years his senior. His next-nearest is older still. There is no one in the house close to his age. He grew up surrounded by adults — adult parents, adult siblings, adult cousins — and he treats the world accordingly. He is not childlike, but he is not artificially old either. He is fifteen with a particular kind of formation.
The Honor
His existence is bound to the Rebirth without his ever having met Lessio. The Locsird boy and the Navori boy share a season of birth, and across the kingdom there are thousands of such children — born in the years when the world chose to answer the cosmic event with new life. Koran is not unusual for being one. He is unusual for being one in this family.
The Weight
Khlora carries him differently. Not loved more — loved differently. After the war, after the three children she lost, after the long deliberate rebuild — to have a final child arrive in the season the world rejoiced is not nothing. She does not say so out loud. She does not need to.
The Betrothal
Sandy Galmir, of Maia's branch, twenty-two years his senior. The arrangement is Maia's instrument — a thread adjacent to a council seat, achieved through Navori blood rather than the Rhill line directly. The match is confirmed; the marriage is deferred until Koran is older. Sandy will be naturally dominant in the dynamic. The age gap will read as easy and organic. There is no forced timeline.

He stands fifteen years old at series start, in deep emerald velvet at his first state evenings, the gold pin at his lapel and his father's pearl ring catching the light. His arc is the longest in the family — he has the most life still ahead of him, and the least so far behind. What he becomes is not yet written.

IV.
Relations
Khlora Rhill
Mother · The Matriarch · The Late Gift's Anchor
She does not love him more than the others. She loves him differently. He arrived after the long rebuild, in the season the world chose to answer the Rebirth with new life. She carries him with the weight of someone who knows what her own grief almost did to her, and who has decided not to let it shape this one. Her hand is on his shoulder in the family photograph for a reason.
Roven Navori
Father · The Cultural Anchor
Roven teaches him the things a Navori son learns from his father. The braiding will come when the beard arrives. The pearl ring will come when the standing is earned. For now, his father stands beside him and does not rush him, and Koran has the rest of his life to grow into the rest of it.
Sandy Galmir
Betrothed · Twenty-Two Years His Senior
The marriage is arranged but deferred. Sandy will lead the dynamic — the age gap requires it and the personalities support it. She is the one who adjusts his collar before he goes downstairs. She is the one who fixes his bow tie before a state event. He stands still and lets her. The match is structural — Maia's instrument, adjacent to the council seat through Navori blood — but what shows up in the room between them is easy. There is no awkwardness in either direction. He has not been asked to be in love with her. No one has asked her to wait for him to grow into it either. They are simply where they are, together, with grace.
Lessio Locsird
Distant Peer · Same Birth Year · The Rebirth
They have not met. They may never meet in any meaningful sense. Both were born in the season the world honored the cosmic event with new life. Lessio is the vessel of that event. Koran is one of thousands of children born in its wake. The connection is structural, not personal. But the season is shared.
The Pre-War Siblings
Khloe, Kole, Maka, Kaya · Adults Long Before His Birth
They were already over a hundred years old when he was born. They are kind to him. They do not perform older-sibling banter — there is no register for that across this kind of gap. They treat him with the same considered, even attention they give other adults. He has never known anything else.
The Post-War Sisters
Rena, Mora, Nika · His Nearest, Still Far
The closest of them in age, Nika, is fifty-nine years his senior. They are the ones who hold him most closely when the family gathers — they are softer with him, less measured, more openly affectionate. They were born in the rebuild, like him. There is something they share with him the eldest cannot fully access.
