I.
Standing

Second daughter of Melyra Galmir and Marcus Azane. Renowned warrior. Exceptional duelist. Unmarried by personal choice — not by Melyra's call.

In a household built on Melyra's blood-child-first framework, Diamyra is the one who has chosen neither. No marriage, no blood child, no active obligation of any kind. The reason is personal, not negotiated. Her mother has not pressed it. The household has not pressed it. The path is hers to walk in her own time, in whichever direction she eventually walks it.

She is, by reputation across the kingdom, one of the finest single combatants in Melyra's generation. She is, in her family, the one with a tournament incident in her past that she will not discuss, and a sister she has not been in the same room with in some time.

II.
Appearance
Build
Tall. Lean through the shoulders. The frame of someone whose body is a tool she has spent decades sharpening.
Hair
One side of her head shaved close to the skin. A single blonde braid running over the other side. The asymmetry is not a fashion choice. It is a duelist's asymmetry — practical, signature, hers.
Face
Sharp-featured. Made to read intention before words arrive.
Bearing
Built like someone who has never once needed a room to go quiet when she enters it. It simply does.
Eyes
Move with the rapid economy of someone taking tactical stock of any space within seconds of entering it.

III.
The Duelist

Canon places her among the exceptional duelists of her generation — singled out by the strategic candidates list as a top-tier match for Damon Martial on combat prestige alone. (The match was not pursued. It would not have suited Melyra's long-term arrangement of the household.) Whatever blade or staff she chooses, the assessment that follows is consistent: she does not waste motion.

Her training is private. Her style is hers. She does not give exhibitions and does not enjoy attention being paid to her work in public-facing settings. The reputation has built itself anyway.

✦ ✦ ✦
IV.
The Tournament Incident

Some years ago, at a tournament whose details are not currently a matter of household record, Diamyra and Dixenthra "Dixie" Vendrith — the daughter of Maia's branch of the family — had something pass between them. The exact shape of the incident is not yet established in canon. Its weight is.

It is the reason Diamyra is absent from Gemstone for the celebration in the opening arc. It is the reason she does not appear in the same rooms as Dixie if she can help it. It is, by extension, one of the active fault lines between Melyra's branch and Maia's — quieter than the matriarchs' own rivalry but no less personal.

The household does not press her on it. Whatever happened, Diamyra is the one who decides when, or whether, it stops mattering.

VI.
Relations
Melyra Galmir
Mother · Matriarch
Has not pressed her on the marriage question. Has not pressed her on the blood-child question. The non-pressing is, itself, a form of trust — and a form of patience Melyra extends to very few.
Myra Galmir
Older Sister
The composed one. The one who has already done what Melyra wanted from her. Diamyra is at peace with the contrast — they are the two ends of what this household allows its daughters to choose.
Myrella Galmir
Younger Sister · The Blood Mother
The closest sister, in every way that matters. When Myrella ran into the fog at Aurora barefoot and unarmed in the opening pages of the series, it was Diamyra who tracked her there at a dead run, on no warning, ready to do whatever the night required. She was furious. She was also already moving.
Dixenthra Vendrith
Cousin · Tournament Rival
Maia's daughter on the other branch. The reason Diamyra is not at Gemstone for the opening arc. The incident at the tournament between them is the unhealed wound of this generation of cousins — and the household, on both sides, mostly knows not to ask.
Damon Martial
Marriage Candidate (Declined)
Considered as a top-tier candidate on combat prestige in the Tyrana negotiation. Not pursued. The negotiation moved instead to Mez-tah Toraiyo through the Crown thread, leaving Diamyra exactly where she had wanted to be in the first place — unattached.
