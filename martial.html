I.
The House
Feared. Respected. Unpredictable.

No other high family on West Island functions the way House Martial does. The others note this. None intervene.

House Martial produces no biological children. Every member of every generation is adopted — selected for Apex potential, raised inside the factional structure, and conditioned into the identity Tyrana requires for each faction's purpose. This is not the norm elsewhere in Gemlyria. Elsewhere, adoption is a supplement. Here it is the only method.

Husbands are acquired through marriage — either negotiated as political instruments or taken as trophies of war. Their autonomy is systematically stripped over time. The chain-wife system governs what happens inside the compound, and other houses do not print the words, but they know the arrangement. Martial's sons are the exception: Tyran, Damon, Mars, and Kassian are adopted young and raised as the house's outbound marriage leverage — the single most desirable candidates on West Island for any family trying to pull Apex blood into its line.

Outward reputation: feared because of the brutality, respected because of the Apex concentration, barely trusted by anyone. Tyrana's Council seat is held. It is not liked. The Crown tolerates Martial because the alternative — a house this strong turning inward in civil war — is worse than the one that exists now.

II.
The Three Factions
Internal rivalry. Outward unity.

The internal geography of Martial is not a single roster. It is three distinct formations, each with its own matriarch, its own rank of daughters, its own acquired husbands, and its own specific purpose inside the house's wider structure. They rival each other openly — within limits that Tyrana alone sets.

The First Line enforces the internal order. Eldest authority, succession, the machinery of discipline. The Spear Sons are the outbound marriage leverage — the four men the kingdom's other houses compete to secure. The Crimson Baronesses are five petty sovereigns each holding her own territorial domain within the Martial seat, running the soft war Martial conducts across the continent's quieter channels.

All three report to Tyrana. None of them harmonize with each other. They are not meant to.
