I.
Physical Appearance

Long jet-black hair, thick, worn loose with a slight wave — falling well past the shoulders. The darkness against the pale of her skin and the black of her Rendering Marks creates a visual register that reads as cold and absolute even in still photographs. Pale eyes — light icy blue, nearly grey. Sharp and direct at close range. No surface tremor in them. Whatever moves in her, moves underneath.

Slender. Tall. Composed. Not visibly combat-coded — the danger is read in stillness, not posture. People who have never seen her use her power often miss what she is until she becomes still in a way that signals it. Even in maternal contexts she does not soften her face — the softness is in proximity and physical care, not in expression. This is not coldness. It is what the War Maker Trials have done to the surface architecture of her face over seven centuries.

Her wardrobe palette is imperial purple, gold, white, cream — the Royal House colors worn as identity, not ceremony. Imperial purple gowns with gold embroidery at formal occasions. Cream and ivory blouses with gold-embroidered collars at home. The same wardrobe in maternal contexts. She does not change for the children. They come to her as she is.

The Rendering Marks — Living Epic
Sonya-Lee carries the Rendering across her entire body — a permanent full-body text in a sacred language no living person can read. Black against pale skin. Face, throat, hands, forearms, torso, legs. Dense script, continuous, not decorative — reads as written language. Present at every occasion. Never concealed. Hiding them would be, in the Understanding, "like hiding the holy Bible." She wears them at formal galas, domestic mornings, maternal moments, council sessions, and battlefields. The marks are not part of how she presents. They are what she is.

When her power is fully brought to bear, the Trials Marks surface beneath the Rendering — a second sacred text in a separate ancient language, burning through and around the first. No one alive can read either. For the average witness, it is the most theologically charged sight available in the world. Almost no one who sees it survives the seeing.

III.
Relations
Jonzhento Locsird
Husband · The Chosen Marriage
He knew the stories before he knew her. He learned what she actually was upon meeting her. He has never asked about the marks. He continues to choose. The marriage succeeded in ways that were not expected of it — and that success is something they made, not something that was available to them by default.
Lessio Locsird
Son · The Rebirth · Doubled Grief
She carries a doubled grief: she lost him at two, and got a different Lessio back. The boy Emerald knew does not exist in his memory. She holds both — what she lost and what she has — simultaneously. The War Maker does not make grief easier to hold. It may make it harder to show.
Samantha-Lilly Toraiyo
Daughter · Tense Dynamic
Tense. The dynamic is real and has its own specific register. Whatever moves between them — over Lessio, over the past, over what Sam carries and how she carries it — has not resolved. They are too alike in certain registers for it to be simple.
Emerald Locsird
Daughter · The Second Mother She Needed
She leaned on Emerald's warmth — the softness the Trials took from her extended parenting. The relationship was symbiotic and real. When Sapphire took both Lessio and Emerald in the same morning, she lost two things simultaneously that she had come to depend on. The containment broke. The world witnessed it and has not forgotten.
Sylvia-Lyn Toraiyo
Mother · Bearer Precedent · West Island
War Maker Elder. Bearer. Council Seat 10 — rarely attends, rarely leaves West Island. The precedent. Sylvia-Lyn is the first Bearer Gemlyria had seen in millennia when she emerged; her daughter is the second. Ruby is on West Island with her grandmother now. Sonya-Lee knows.
Sapphire Toraiyo
Daughter · The Decree
Following Sapphire, she will never permit another of her children to enter the Trials. This is not a preference. It is absolute and non-negotiable. What she will do if Sapphire reemerges is one of the questions that governs every room she enters for the rest of the series.
