I.
Who She Was

Emerald was the originally designated Heir — the role that passed to Ayravelle only by the accident of her death. She was warm, nurturing, entirely at ease with the infant Lessio in a way that bypassed politics and prophecy entirely. She treated him as simply her brother. Not as the Rebirth. Not as a cosmic event. As her brother.

Sonya-Lee leaned on this dynamic. The War Maker transformation strips the extended softness of daily parenting — Emerald provided that, naturally and without being asked. She was the one Lessio reached for. The one who carried him. The one who settled him when nothing else did.

She died at 87 on the morning of Lessio's second birthday, attempting to stop Sapphire from taking him. She was killed in the attempt.

◆ ◆ ◆
II.
Physical Appearance

Long, voluminous blonde hair — worn loose and curled in semi-formal settings, worn up in a high curled ponytail at formal events. Light blue eyes. Slender, poised build. Confident and warm bearing, entirely at ease in high-society settings. Her wardrobe palette mirrors the Royal House exactly: white, imperial purple, gold.

IV.
The Absence She Left

Lessio has no memory of her. Not her face. Not the warmth. Not the way she carried him. He processes her death as a historical tragedy, not a personal loss — and that blankness is one of the quieter wounds the family carries. Everyone who loved her watches him encounter her absence like a stranger.

Sonya-Lee's grief is doubled: she lost Lessio at two, and got a different Lessio back. The boy Emerald knew does not exist in his memory. The Lessio who returned was not shaped by her warmth — because the years in which that warmth would have formed him were taken from him entirely.

Lessio may carry an unnameable absence — a hollowness whose origin he cannot identify, shaped in part by the loss of the person who was simply, without agenda, his.

V.
Relations
Lessio Locsird
Brother · No Memory
She was his second mother before he had language for it. She died protecting him. He has no memory of her. His blankness is not indifference — it is the shape of the fourteen years that were taken from him.
Sonya-Lee Toraiyo
Mother · Doubled Grief
Sonya-Lee leaned on Emerald's warmth — the softness the Trials stripped from her parenting. She lost both her son and Emerald in the same morning. She did not resist the second-mother dynamic. She needed it. Its loss compounds everything.
Ayravelle Locsird
Sister · Inherited Her Role
The Heir role was Emerald's by design — the Triplet Exception, Locsird name. Ayravelle inherited it by circumstance. She was never built for it from birth. The burden she carries is partly the weight of the role that should have been Emerald's.
Aurea-Lee Locsird
Sister · The Last to Hold Her
Aurea-Lee was six. She was napping in the same room. She hid under the bed. When the noise stopped she crawled out and was found clinging to Emerald's body. She was the last person to hold her. She has carried this for fourteen years without speaking it aloud to anyone.
