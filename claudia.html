I.
Standing

Third of Maia's daughters. Betrothed to Aldren Solarn of House Solarn, Astravar — old nobility with deep roots and Stellgaze-era ties.

Claudia is the branch's reach across the continent. Where Maini consolidates home ground and Margaret and Sandy hold the Martinhal–Rhill corridor, Claudia projects outward: into Astravar, into House Solarn's old history with Stellgaze, into territory that Rhyzen Locsird considers his backyard.

The Tyrana Marriage Proposal document ranked Claudia as Maia's primary candidate across every considered match — the strongest realistic diplomatic bond, the daughter who would have given Martial a direct line to Maia's political machine. Maia kept her for Astravar instead. That choice tells you what Astravar was worth in Maia's ledger.

III.
The Astravar Marriage

Aldren Solarn carries the warrior disposition of House Solarn — capable on a battlefield, proud in bearing, steady in a fight. His age is not yet locked in canon. What is locked: Claudia dominates the marriage and the betrothal outright. Politically, socially, administratively, she runs the relationship. Aldren is capable where it matters in a duel and outflanked everywhere else.

Maia's motive is explicit in the strategic summary: a Galmir foothold in Astravar, a deniable but deliberate provocation of Rhyzen Locsird. Astravar is Stellgaze territory by long tradition. Placing a Galmir daughter into House Solarn — old Astravar blood — is the kind of move that is easy to justify and impossible to ignore.

Rhyzen has noted it. Rhyzen will not confront it directly. The provocation is the point.
