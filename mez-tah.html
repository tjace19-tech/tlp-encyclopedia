I.
Personality & Character

Fiery. Confident. Quick-tempered, holds grudges, has a sharp side — but the dominant register at series start is happy. Lessio's return has made her lighter in a way that is visible and genuine. She is less careful with her words than Rhyca, more likely to say something direct in the controlled tension of the convoy vehicle. Her joy cuts occasionally against the heavier undercurrents others are carrying — not from insensitivity but from real gladness.

Of the non-War Maker sisters, she is second only to Samantha-Lilly in terms of skill with a blade. Athletic, combat-trained, toned. She takes rotating driving shifts alongside Rhyca on the convoy road to Aurora.

She was 21 on the day of the abduction. She was thrown from the second story stairs through the coffee table below by Sapphire. She came around first — within minutes — and found Sam bleeding in a pool of her own blood, eyes barely open, muttering that Sapphire had taken the baby. She sat conscious and injured watching Sam bleed until the King and Queen arrived. Neither she nor Sam has ever spoken of what passed between them in that moment. Both know. Both know the other knows.

III.
Relations
Damon Martial
Betrothed · Crown Arrangement
The Crown-initiated thread in the Tyrana betrothal arrangement — Jonzhento arranged Mez-tah and Damon as the Royal House's stake in the Martial alliance. Damon is 18, calm, quiet, intimidating, with first-peak Apex potential. The dynamic between them has not yet been developed.
Samantha-Lilly Toraiyo
Sister · The Shared Scar
The eye contact when Mez came around — Sam barely alert, blood everywhere — is the totality of what was ever communicated between them about that morning. It lives underneath their general sibling dynamic permanently. They are the only two who know exactly what those final minutes looked like.
Rhyca Locsird
Sister · Close · Complementary
Close despite being fundamentally different people. Rhyca is patient and warm; Mez is fiery and quick. They complement rather than mirror. In the convoy vehicle they take the driving together, trading off — two sisters who cover different registers of the same family.
Lessio Locsird
Brother · The Return
His return made her lighter. Whatever weight she has carried from the abduction day — and she carries it, she was there and remembers exactly what she woke to — his coming home offsets something. Her joy in the convoy is the clearest expression of this in Chronicle One.
