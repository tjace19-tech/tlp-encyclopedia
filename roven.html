I.
Personality
The presence beside her.

Roven does not perform. He does not dominate rooms. He does not recede into the corners of them either. He is the man Khlora comes home to at the end of council days, and what he offers her is the absence of demand. He has never asked her to be smaller. He has never asked her to be less of what she is.

His expression in any room — formal, casual, the field at dawn — is the same. Calm. Focused. Steady. He is not measuring himself against her seat. The seat is hers and Roven's house standing is his own, separate matter — House Navori has provincial weight in Rhill territory, and he is the most prominent male figure of it. That is enough for him. It has always been enough.

The thing other houses sometimes get wrong about him: they read his quietness as deference. It is not. Roven does not raise his voice because he has never needed to. The people in his orbit know exactly where he stands and what he thinks. He simply does not require them to know it loudly.

II.
The Cultural Anchor
Plains heritage worn deliberately.

Roven is what Rhill heritage looks like on a man who has never been at war with the modern world for the right to keep it. He drives a Gem Engine. He wears a tailored coat. He stands beside Khlora at state evenings in emerald and gold and looks like he was born for the cut. None of that competes with what he carries from the plains. They sit on him at the same time.

The Beard
Worked with small braids running through the lower half. Threaded with dark wooden beads — and a single iridescent pearl bead, drawn from the Plains Predator. The pearl marks his full cultural standing within House Navori. He braids it himself. The braids are not vanity. They are the work he does on himself.
The Pearl Ring
Worn on the right hand across every context — casual, battle, formal. The Plains Predator pearl is the most valuable artifact the region produces. It is never gifted outside Rhill or Rue. The ring on Roven's hand is a statement that does not need to be spoken: he belongs here, fully and without qualification.
The Doctrine
Rhill keeps two registers — emerald and gold for being seen, earth tones for the field. Roven moves between both without hesitation. In formal dress he is Rhill in public. In battle dress he is Rhill in private. The bronze metalwork is the only constant — the cultural signature that survives the palette shift.

The casual register is the one outsiders rarely see and never fully read. Long fur-trimmed leather coat, hair loose, the beard catching the morning wind, the modern hillside estate at his back. This is the man at home. Khlora is somewhere inside, or somewhere on the council circuit, or somewhere on the drill field with Titano Rue. Roven is here, where she will return to. That is where he stays. That is where he has stayed for one hundred and forty years.

IV.
Relations
Khlora Rhill
Wife · The Matriarch · The Constant
Forty-one years his junior on paper, peer in every way Apex lifespan makes age irrelevant for. They met on the same battlefield, and what came after the battle was the rest of their lives. He has never tried to lead the province. She has never asked him to. The partnership is not symmetrical and never tried to be — it is exact, and it has held for over a century.
Koran Navori
Youngest Son · Rebirth-Era · The Late Gift
The unexpected eighth child. Born when Roven was already 314 years old and the daughters had been grown a long time. He is fifteen. Roven teaches him the things a Navori son learns from his father — the braiding, the pearl, the standing — and waits for the rest of the boy's life to arrive in its own time. Koran stands between his parents in every public photograph. That is not stagecraft. That is where he naturally goes.
Kole Navori
Eldest Surviving Son · Pre-War
The son who came back from the war. Roven lost a son in the same conflict, and Kole carries that for both of them now. The Navori name continues through Kole. Roven does not name the brother who did not return. Neither does Kole.
The Daughters
Khloe, Maka, Kaya · Rena, Mora, Nika
Six surviving daughters, four pre-war and three post-war. Roven was beside Khlora through every one of them. He does not raise daughters the way some plains men do — he does not perform sternness, does not stage approval. He is steady, and they know what they have in him. He does not need to perform the rest.
House Navori
Provincial House · Rhill Territory
Not a council-level house. Not a Matriarch's seat. Provincial standing within Rhill — established, respected, his. The line continues through the children who carry the Navori name by birth-order alternation. House Meshari is reserved for future use; House Navori is what Roven is responsible for now.
Sandy Galmir
Future Daughter-in-Law · Maia's Branch
Betrothed to Koran. Twenty-two years his senior on paper, peer once Koran reaches his stride. The match is Maia Galmir's instrument — adjacent to the council seat through Navori blood, not the Rhill line directly. Roven understands what the placement means. He has not contested it.
