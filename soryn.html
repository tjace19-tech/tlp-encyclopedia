I.
Personality
Her father's fuse, her mother's ink.

Soryn is the rowdy one. She inherited her father's temper rather than her mother's stillness, and it shows in everything from how she enters a room to how she leaves one. Where Maraveth listens to the room and what is moving underneath it, Soryn picks a fight with the room directly. Where Calistra carries the weight of being eldest with composure, Soryn carries the freedom of being last with both hands and is not always careful with it.

She has the streaked hair on purpose — black with platinum running through it, the cosmic field made literal. It was not subtle when she chose it. It was not meant to be. The constellation tattoos extend across her sleeves, neck, hands. She wears the Stellgaze register completely, and she wears it loud. There is no quiet version of Soryn at a state function.

The temper is genuine. Quick to spark, slower to cool. The siblings closest to her age have learned to read the warnings — Dravik in particular knows when to step in front of her and when to let her go. The court has learned the same lesson the slower way. She is thirty-five, which by Apex measure is barely past adolescence, and she has not yet decided that she should soften any of this. Whether she ever will is an open question. Her parents have stopped trying to pre-decide the answer.

II.
The Quick Fuse
The temperament she did not inherit from her mother.
Source
Her father. Rhyzen carries the sardonic version of the same temperament — irritated by formality, unwilling to soften for the room. Soryn took the underlying shape and removed the sardonic distance. The result is louder.
Triggers
Condescension. Being talked over. The presumption that the youngest in the room is the least to be reckoned with. Anyone treating her name as a junior version of her mother's. She has corrected each of these the hard way at least once.
Restraint
Not natural to her. What she has is choice — she can hold her temper when she decides to. She does not always decide to. The line between strategic patience and refusing to bother is not always visible from outside.
In the Field
The temper translates. In battle dress she is dangerous in a different register than her sisters — Calistra fights the way she thinks, Soryn fights the way she feels, and both are deadly for opposite reasons. The cosmic armor reads the same on her as on the others. The woman inside it does not.

IV.
Relations
Maraveth Stellgaze
Mother · The Matriarch · The Patient One
They are not alike. Soryn knows it. Maraveth knows it. The relationship works because Maraveth has chosen — deliberately, and over decades — not to require Soryn to become a quieter version of herself. The far-seeing did not pass to Soryn the way it might pass to Calistra. What Soryn inherited from her mother is the refusal to flinch. Different register. Same root.
Rhyzen Locsird
Father · The Same Fuse
The temperament came from him. He sees it, recognizes it, and has not pretended otherwise. He does not lecture her about it. He has, on occasion, stepped between her and a worse confrontation by drawing the heat onto himself — sardonic where she would be loud — and she has noticed every time. The bond is real and uncomplicated. It does not need to be discussed.
Calistra Stellgaze
Eldest Sister · The Composed One
Fifty-two years between them. Calistra is the eldest who carries weight; Soryn is the youngest who refuses to. The difference does not produce conflict between them — it produces a kind of mutual amusement. Calistra has bailed Soryn out of more situations than either of them has admitted to their parents. Soryn has caught Calistra at her one unguarded moment more than once. Each of them keeps the other's confidence. They have never agreed to. They simply do.
Dravik Locsird
Brother · Two Years Apart · The Steadier
The closest sibling in age, and the one who has learned to read her warnings best. He is the steady one in the pair — quiet where she is loud, considered where she is reactive — and he has stepped in front of her at least three times when stepping in front would cost him something. She has never thanked him in plain language for any of it. They both know.
King Jonzhento Locsird
Uncle · The Crown
Her father's brother. He has known her since she was born, treats her with the warm familiarity of family rather than the careful deference of a king. He is not afraid of her temper. She is not impressed by his crown. The two attitudes have produced a relationship that is closer to genuine than any of her court interactions ever get.
Lessio Locsird
Cousin · The Returned One
She does not know him well. He is sixteen, just back into the family, and the weight of what is expected of him is the kind of weight that has never been placed on her. Whether that weight would crush her or detonate her if it ever were is a question she has never had to answer. She watches him at family functions with a kind of careful neutrality. He has not yet given her a reason to land on a verdict. She is in no hurry.
