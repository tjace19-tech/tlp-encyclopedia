I.
Standing

Infant son of Myra Galmir and Titano Rue. Roughly four to five months old at series start. Confirmed Apex.

Myrin is the present-tense fulfillment of Melyra's framework — the blood child that closed his mother's lineage obligation, the proof of concept for the household's entire structural philosophy. He is, in the language of the household, what comes of doing it the right way.

He is also, at four to five months, an infant. Too young for any active role in the events of the opening arc. Present at Aurora under his mother's arms and his grandmother's roof.

✦ ✦ ✦
II.
The Name

His last name is Galmir, per the alternating naming convention — Galmir takes the name when it is the more powerful line, and on a confirmed Apex infant of the Galmir-Azane branch, Galmir is unambiguously the more powerful line.

Melyra and Myra both offered, formally, to give him the name Rue instead — breaking the convention deliberately, in honor of Titano's position as the last living member of his house and the only person who could continue that line. Titano declined. The decline was respectful, complete, and final. The family did not press it.

✦ ✦ ✦
III.
What He Reveals

Myrin's presence is, in narrative function, the visible proof that the Galmir-Azane household has warmer dimensions than the matriarch's reputation suggests. The way Myra holds him, the way Diamyra and Maylyra orbit him in the household's quieter hours, the way Melyra herself softens around the cradle — these are facts about this family that the rest of the kingdom does not see.

He is the household's small, sleeping reminder that the framework is not theoretical. The blood-child philosophy produced him. He is what it produced.
