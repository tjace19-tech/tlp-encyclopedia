I.
The Branch
Eldest by blood. Not by law.

There are two Galmir branches. This is the one with the wings.

Melyra Galmir is the eldest of all Galmir children — the firstborn of Rhorlyn Galmir and Myrel of Myrient — but she is not the eldest legitimate heir. That distinction belongs to her younger half-sister Maia, born of a formal political marriage. The line between *eldest* and *eldest legitimate* is the foundation of a rivalry that has compounded across centuries.

After the war's end and her father's death, Melyra did not contest Lake Port. She left it. She lived briefly with Marcus Azane long enough to bear her children, then established her own estate on the coast of Aurora — *Myrient's Grace* — a deliberate memorial tribute to the ancient Novan whose blood she carries. The silver-and-platinum wings on the estate gate are her own sigil, claimed by her hand alone, formally distinguishing her line from the wider Galmir family.

The wings appear in her battle armor, in the ornamentation of the house, in the embroidery her daughters wear. They are the visual shorthand for what this branch is: kin to Galmir, but its own thing.

II.
Blood Children First
Melyra's philosophy, locked in canon.

Melyra was a blood child herself — her entire branch exists because of that arrangement. She has built her household around the same principle in reverse: her daughters fulfill their lineage obligation through blood children before any formal marriage is considered. Marriage, when it comes, is personal. The arcane legacy is the priority.

This is the deliberate structural opposite of Maia's branch. Where Maia treats her daughters' marriages as instruments of statecraft — every alliance arranged, every condition negotiated, every house penetrated — Melyra treats hers as private decisions to be made after a different obligation has been honored. The two matriarchs have never tried to harmonize the two systems. They are not meant to harmonize.

The result, in the present generation, is a household where Myra has fulfilled her obligation and is free, Diamyra has chosen no path at all, Myrella sits at the absolute center of a coexistence arrangement involving the Crown itself, Maylyra is being quietly prospected for, and Mimyr has not yet been brought into the conversation.
