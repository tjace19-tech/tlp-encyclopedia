I.
Standing

Eldest child of Rhorlyn Galmir and Myrel of Myrient. Matriarch of her own branch of the Galmir family. Carrier of the Blood of Myrient. War legend. Architect of the household at Aurora.

By blood, Melyra is the eldest of all Galmir children — born of her father's first relationship, to a woman whose own bloodline ran back to the ancient Novan Myrient through generations of mortal Apex descent. She is older than Maia by two centuries and stronger than her by orders of magnitude, and she is, by formal political reckoning, not the eldest legitimate heir. Maia holds that distinction. Maia keeps it.

Melyra did not contest it. After the Hadean War, after her father's death in the Great Surge, after the political reordering that came with Gemlyria's unification — she walked away from Lake Port. She took nothing of the ancestral seat with her. She built her own house on the Auroran coastline, named it for her mother's ancient line, put the wings on the gate, and did not look back.

The rivalry with Maia compounds across centuries because Melyra is not in it. She is somewhere else, doing something else, and the doing of it is its own answer to every claim her younger half-sister has ever made about the family name.

III.
The Blood of Myrient

The Blood of Myrient is a once-in-a-generation gift inherited from Melyra's mother — itself inherited from Myrel's line, running back to the ancient Novan Myrient through generations of mortal Apex descent. Rhorlyn did not carry it. The Galmir name does not carry it. Maia's entire branch is without it. It runs through Myrel's blood alone.

What it grants the carrier:

— Enhanced raw arcane power, beyond what the Apex tier alone allows.
— Exceptional combat perception — the ability to anticipate an enemy's attack, or block, before they have moved.

In Melyra, the gift expressed at full force, layered over centuries of combat experience, refined into something that the rest of the kingdom's warriors learned to feel before they could see. It skipped past her firstborn, Myra, and surfaced again in her second daughter, Myrella — confirming what the household had already suspected: the gift does not follow strict birth order. It chooses.

IV.
The Wings of Myrient

After the war's end and her departure from Lake Port, Melyra chose her own sigil. Not a derivation of the standard Galmir device — something entirely her own. Dual silver-and-platinum wings, full-spread, mirrored. The reference is direct: Myrient, the most storied ancient White, classically rendered in the visual record with great pale wings extended. Melyra took the wings and made them hers.

They appear on the gate of her estate in Aurora — an enormous architectural rendering, blue-and-silver against the white marble of the gatehouse. They appear in her battle armor, worked into the breastplate. They appear in the embroidery her daughters wear at formal occasions when the household is being represented as itself rather than as a piece of the wider Galmir line.

The wings are not decoration. They are the formal declaration that this branch of the family is its own thing — kin to Galmir, but answerable to itself.

V.
The Architect of the Household

Melyra has built her household around a single structural principle: blood children first, marriage second. She was a blood child herself, and her entire branch exists because of that arrangement. Her daughters carry the same framework forward: the lineage obligation comes first, the formal political marriage comes second, and the second is left to the daughter's personal discretion entirely.

The system runs in deliberate opposition to her half-sister's. Where Maia treats her own daughters as instruments of statecraft — every alliance arranged, every house penetrated, the family map redrawn marriage by marriage — Melyra treats hers as people who owe their bloodline a contribution and themselves the rest. The two systems do not harmonize. They are not meant to.

In the present generation, the framework has produced what Melyra wanted it to produce: Myra has fulfilled, with Myrin in her arms; Diamyra has chosen no path at all and the household supports that choice; Myrella sits at the absolute center of the most consequential blood-child arrangement the kingdom has ever seen; Maylyra is being prospected for; Mimyr has not yet been brought into the conversation. Each daughter where she is supposed to be. Each daughter on her own time.

✦ ✦ ✦
VI.
The Compact with the Crown

Melyra and Jonzhento Locsird fought side by side through the Hadean War. The bond between them is deep, personal, and predates everything else either of them has built. When Lessio washed up wounded on the beach at Aurora — when the beach fog cleared and Myrella was carrying him toward the house and Diamyra was sweeping the perimeter for the threat that never came — it was Melyra who performed the healing on her own dining table, and Melyra who composed the cryptic message to Gemstone for the King's eyes only.

The Blood Child arrangement that now binds Lessio to Myrella is, at the highest level, an arrangement between Melyra and the Crown. The negotiation runs through her hands. The household is hers. The compact holds because the trust between her and Jonzhento was earned in the worst war either of them ever fought, and it has never broken since.
