I.
Standing

The eldest legitimate heir of House Galmir. Daughter of Rhorlyn Galmir and the formal political marriage he entered after Myrel of Myrient's passing. Sole Head of House. Two centuries younger than her half-sister Melyra by blood, and the one the law recognizes.

Maia holds the seat. Maia holds the keep. Maia holds the title. After the war's end and Rhorlyn's death, Melyra voluntarily left Grandholm — did not contest it, did not fight for it. Maia absorbed Lake Port, the island, and the century-long administrative reach of the Galmir name without making a move. The rivalry between the two half-sisters is defined by what Melyra surrendered, not by what Maia took.

She was the legitimate heir before she was anything else. Everything she built afterward was built on that foundation.

II.
Appearance
Hair
Long, pale blonde, waved and styled with unfailing precision. Worn decorated in formal settings — ornamental hairpins, braided structures. Never once unstyled in public in three centuries.
Eyes
Pale blue. Direct.
Build
Tall, elegantly proportioned, carries herself with the settled authority of a woman who has not been contradicted in any serious matter for longer than most high-family members have been alive.
Bearing
Composed at rest; commanding in motion. Hands-on-hips is her natural posture — not performed, not cultivated, just how her body organizes itself when she is thinking.
Wardrobe
House colors across every register — royal blue, white, gold. Formal wear leans toward velvet and heavy embroidered gold work. Casual wear leans toward structured tailoring and signature rings on every finger.

III.
Personality

Cunning, poised, politically ruthless. The core canon descriptors do not soften with nuance — they are accurate. She is not warm. She is not performatively cold either. She is the calibrated register of a matriarch who has concluded, over three centuries, that most people overestimate their importance and most situations resolve themselves if observed rather than intervened in.

Her intelligence runs in two distinct directions at once: immediate-read tactical awareness (who in the room matters, who is about to speak, what the stakes of the next exchange actually are) and decade-horizon structural awareness (what a given marriage will mean for her grandchildren's grandchildren, what concession extracted now will compound into leverage in sixty years). She holds both frames simultaneously and neither degrades the other.

She does not monologue. She does not explain her thinking to her daughters and she does not explain it to her peers. Her moves are self-evident after the fact and inscrutable before it. The few people who have tried to read her in real time (Tyrana Martial, Rhyzen Locsird, Jonzhento on occasion) have each come away from the encounter with the suspicion that they had been told exactly as much as she wanted them to know.

IV.
The Architect of Alliances

The current generation of the kingdom is shaped by Maia's betrothal map more than any other single influence outside the Crown. Every one of her daughters carries a strategic designation. The threads reach into every politically significant province in Gemlyria:

Dardena
Maini → Lyle Helsird. Home-province consolidation. Before Maia reached anywhere else, she locked down the ground beneath her own keep.
Astravar
Claudia → Aldren Solarn. Deniable provocation of Rhyzen Locsird. Old Astravar blood penetrated by a Galmir daughter.
Martinhal
Margaret → Kaelyn Valdren. Old-money, old-combat-tradition thread into Martinhal. Placed alongside Rhyca Locsird with pre-existing friction intact.
Rhill
Sandy → Koran Navori. Council-adjacent thread — one step from Khlora Rhill's voting seat. The deferred marriage gives Sandy the easiest life and Maia the quietest leverage.
Martial
Jackie → Mars Martial. Tyrana asked for a Galmir and received a Vendrith with three attached conditions. The quiet humiliation is now a filed debt.
The Crown
Holly → Lessio Locsird. The oldest betrothal on the map — arranged before cosmology made it historic. Maia will have a granddaughter raised in the Royal Household.

Seven threads. Seven provinces touched. No two daughters placed redundantly. This is not a collection of marriages. It is a lattice, and Maia is the node at its center.

✦ ✦ ✦
V.
The Rivalry with Melyra

Maia and Melyra are half-sisters. Their father Rhorlyn had Melyra first, in his youth, by a non-political relationship with Myrel of Myrient — a once-in-a-generation blood-of-Myrient bearer. Melyra is 531 years old. Maia is 327. Birth order is not the contested question between them. Legitimacy is.

Melyra holds everything Maia will never touch: the Blood of Myrient, the war-legend reputation, the arcane lineage running back to the oldest cosmology in the world. Maia holds everything Melyra gave up: the seat, the keep, the name in the law books, the sole Head of House designation. Neither half can be exchanged for the other. The line between them has compounded for two centuries, and every generation of Galmir-line children has inherited some version of it.

Maia's response to her half-sister is not grievance. It is quiet, compounding accounting. She notices what Melyra does. She notices who visits Aurora and in what order. She notices that Myrella — Melyra's daughter, carrier of the Blood of Myrient — is the mother of the Rebirth's firstborn son, and she notices that that son will carry the Locsird name by Royal House rule. Maia also notices that Holly's daughter, Maitee, will carry Galmir.

Not openly. Not in words. But in her ledger, the cosmically significant child is not a Galmir, and the Galmir name survives to the next generation by way of her own granddaughter. These things balance in Maia's ledger without being spoken aloud.

VII.
The Quiet Ledger
Power · Prestige · Politics

The Vendrith tagline is not a motto she recites. It is the three-word description of what she has built and what she maintains. Power is the Apex authority she holds personally and the institutional weight of her branch. Prestige is the political reach of her daughters. Politics is the daily work — the conditions, the concessions, the filed humiliations on the other side of every negotiation.

She does not advertise a sigil. She does not need one. The white marble keep of Grandholm, rising from its island in the lake, visible from every approach, is her statement. Royal blue, white, and gold on her daughters' bodies is her visual register. That is enough. Anyone who needs a sigil explained to them was not the audience she was speaking to in the first place.

Maia's husband by formal marriage. Carries the Vendrith name into the household — the second half of the branch's alternating naming convention. Holds no independent political position; his role is stable presence beside Maia, not parallel influence. Age 242.
Melyra Galmir

Eldest. Placed first, into the home province, to lock the ground before reaching outward. The daughter Maia can trust without hedging.

Daughter · Legion
The one Maia could not place. Legion status removes her from the political map entirely — a daughter whose life belongs to an institution Maia cannot negotiate with.
Claudia Galmir

Sandy Galmir
Daughter · Rhill Thread
The daughter given the easiest life and the most durable leverage. Adjacency to a council seat through a deferred marriage — Maia's quietest win.
Jackie Vendrith

Holly Galmir

Asked for a Galmir by name. Accepted a Vendrith with three conditions. Maia does not expect Tyrana to forget. Maia does not need her to.

The arranged marriage between Holly and Lessio precedes the Rebirth's return. Jonzhento and Maia negotiated the match decades before cosmology elevated it. The relationship between them is formal, mutually respectful, and carefully maintained.
